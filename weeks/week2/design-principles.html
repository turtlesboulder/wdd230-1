Hick's Law

  • 4kdownload
  • [4kdownload site]

Hick's Law basically states that the more stimuli a person has, the longer it will take for them to make a decision. Ideally websites have low amounts of stimulus so that the user can quickly make the desired desicion. This website tries to sell a youtube to mp3 converter to the user, and it is uninteresting and white except for the two colorful buttons they want the user to click!

Fitt's Law

  • Ookla, LLC
  • [Ookla speedtest site]

Fitt's law states that it takes longer for a user to reach a target (a button or something they want to mouse over or click on) the further away the target is, and the smaller the target is. In this speedtest website, the button to activate the test is quite large and in the middle of the screen, likely where the user's mouse or finger would already be.

White Space and Clean Design

  • Disney
  • [Disney movies site]

If elements are cluttered together on a webpage, it can be hard for a user to discern elements apart and the amount of information on screen at once can be overwhelming. Disney's website does a good job of leaving plenty of space below the main title of the page, and plenty of room inbetween each picture that represents a movie.
